### **Market Context**
*   **Current Status:** The session closed lower, with the Nasdaq 100 down nearly 1% and the S&P 500 down just shy of 0.6%.
*   **The Cause:** A significant portion of the decline is attributed to geopolitical tensions involving the closure of the **Strait of Hormuz**. Investors are worried about the duration of the closure and the resulting impact on global oil prices.
*   **The Outlook:** March is looking to be a difficult month for equities. The market is currently in "correction territory" but not yet deep enough to be considered a full-blown crash, though nervousness is high.

### **Interview with Rob Heyworth (U.S. Bank Asset Management)**
Rob Heyworth, a Senior Investments Strategy Director, discussed how to navigate this uncertainty:

**1. Where to "Hide" (Defensive Sectors)**
*   **Global Infrastructure:** Specifically **Master Limited Partnerships (MLPs)** and utilities have performed well. These sectors provide stability when energy prices are volatile.
*   **Energy:** While the broader market is shaky, energy stocks themselves have been a relative safe haven within this specific trade.
*   **Fixed Income:** There are attractive yield opportunities (unseen in a decade) in structured credit, non-agency residential mortgage-backed securities (RMBS), and catastrophe bonds.

**2. Offense: Long-Term Trends**
*   **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Despite the short-term noise, AI remains a major driver for the U.S. economy and utility demand.
*   **Consumer Resilience:** As long as unemployment stays low (jobless claims remain reasonable) and retail sales hold up, the consumer can absorb higher energy costs. The U.S. economy is resilient.

**3. Market Psychology**
*   **Patient vs. Complacent:** Heyworth argues the market isn't complacent but rather **"patient."** Investors are rotating out of tech/AI fears (from early in the year) into cyclical sectors and small caps, waiting for a resolution to the Strait of Hormuz crisis.
*   **The Binary Event:** The market hinges on one thing: **When does the Strait reopen?**
    *   If oil stays below ~$70/barrel and the Strait reopens soon, it becomes a buying opportunity.
    *   If prices spike to $85/barrel or more due to prolonged closure, it will dampen consumer spending.

### **Key Takeaways for Investors**
*   **Don't Panic:** The market is reacting to a specific geopolitical event rather than a fundamental breakdown of the U.S. economy.
*   **Diversify:** Use this time to build diversification through global infrastructure and fixed income.
*   **Watch the Data:** The upcoming **Friday Jobs Report** is critical. Strong employment data will confirm consumer resilience, validating the strategy of holding equities while waiting for the energy crisis to resolve.
