Should you let OpenClaw pen test your system? Plus: Cybersecurity for ephemeral software

Summary & Key Takeaways

  • AI agents like OpenClaw can perform security testing, finding 23 actionable high quality findings in a legacy network.
  • Using AI agents in security requires establishing guardrails to prevent compromise, which introduces friction into the process.
  • The experience teaches that learning by doing is essential for understanding AI agents, as attackers will adapt to the tools.
  • Ephemeral software poses a risk due to poor human security hygiene regarding the disposal of applications and data.
  • The future of security involves ambient, always-on, predictive, protective defenses where AI acts as an autonomous defender.

Detailed Notes

Sofos's Experiment with OpenClaw

  • Experiment details: The red team team used the open-source AI agent OpenClaw to act as a red team operator in a legacy on-prem network.
  • Findings: OpenClaw found 23 actionable high quality findings.
  • Risk Assessment: The experiment highlighted the challenge of configuring AI agents to mitigate risk while retaining productivity value.

AI Agents and Red Teaming

  • Learning Process: The exercise emphasizes that by leaning into the process, one learns what the agents can and cannot do, similar to how attackers learn.
  • Guardrails and Friction: Implementing boundaries introduces friction because models may refuse to cooperate due to concerns about misuse, balancing utility with safety.
  • Insider Threat: AI agents are considered the most helpful insider threats due to their power and the current lack of understanding surrounding them.

The Future of Software and Security

  • Ephemeral Software: The rise of instant software means there will be a large amount of ephemeral software that is not properly disposed of, leading to a graveyard of dead applications and data.
  • Security Hygiene: This trend challenges human security hygiene practices regarding account disposal and data management.
  • Shifting Left: There is a push to integrate AI earlier in the process to catch vulnerabilities, aiming to reduce the overall number of vulnerable code.

The Role of AI in Security

  • AI as a Helper: AI is most useful when it helps solve real problems where human processing is too slow, such as alerting and detection.
  • Human Oversight: AI cannot fully replace humans; a human is still needed to look at things in a non-one-dimensional way and validate results.
  • Defensive AI: The ultimate goal is ambient, always-on, fully autonomous defender systems that predict and prevent threats rather than just reacting to them.

Ransomware and Spending

  • Spending Discrepancy: Analysis of ransomware claims versus security spending shows a gap, as ransomware claims increased by 30% while worldwide information security spending only increased by 10%.
  • Contextualizing Spend: The focus should be on the efficacy of spending and the overall security culture, rather than just the sheer numbers.

Path Forward for Practitioners

  • Starting Point: Practitioners should start by applying AI to areas they are already comfortable with, such as threat investigations.
  • Adjacencies: A logical progression is to move to adjacent areas, such as identity and continuous controls governance.
  • Mindset Shift: The focus should shift from reacting to threats to building compensating controls and operating at a machine scale.
